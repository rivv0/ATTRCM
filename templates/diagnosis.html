Diagnosis of ATTR-CM

Methods and approaches for detecting Amyloid Transthyretin Cardiomyopathy

Diagnostic Challenges

ATTR-CM is often underdiagnosed or misdiagnosed for several reasons:

  • Symptoms overlap with more common forms of heart failure
  • Low awareness among healthcare providers
  • Historically required invasive testing (endomyocardial biopsy)
  • Varied clinical presentation

The Diagnostic Pathway

Diagnosis of ATTR-CM typically follows this pathway:

  1. Clinical suspicion based on symptoms and risk factors
  2. Non-invasive cardiac imaging
  3. Exclusion of light chain (AL) amyloidosis
  4. Nuclear scintigraphy or biopsy confirmation
  5. Genetic testing to differentiate between hereditary and wild-type ATTR-CM

Diagnostic Methods

Electrocardiogram (ECG)

While not specific for ATTR-CM, ECGs may show characteristic findings that raise suspicion:

  • Low voltage in limb leads despite increased wall thickness on imaging
  • Pseudoinfarct patterns (Q waves without history of myocardial infarction)
  • Atrial fibrillation or other arrhythmias
  • Conduction abnormalities (e.g., atrioventricular blocks)

Echocardiography

Echocardiography is often the first imaging test that raises suspicion for cardiac amyloidosis:

  • Increased left ventricular wall thickness
  • Diastolic dysfunction
  • Preserved ejection fraction (in early stages)
  • "Sparkling" or "granular" appearance of the myocardium
  • Thickened valves
  • Atrial enlargement
  • Pericardial effusion

Cardiac Magnetic Resonance Imaging (CMR)

CMR with gadolinium contrast can provide additional diagnostic information:

  • Characteristic pattern of late gadolinium enhancement
  • Abnormal gadolinium kinetics
  • Difficulty nulling the myocardium
  • Increased extracellular volume (ECV)

Nuclear Scintigraphy

Bone-seeking radiotracers such as technetium-99m pyrophosphate (99mTc-PYP), 99mTc-DPD, or 99mTc-HMDP have high affinity for TTR amyloid fibrils:

  • Grade 2 or 3 cardiac uptake (equal to or greater than rib uptake)
  • High sensitivity and specificity for ATTR-CM
  • Can diagnose ATTR-CM non-invasively when combined with absence of monoclonal protein

Nuclear scintigraphy showing increased cardiac uptake characteristic of ATTR-CM

Biopsy

Historically, endomyocardial biopsy was the gold standard for diagnosis:

  • Direct visualization of amyloid deposits with Congo red staining
  • Immunohistochemistry or mass spectrometry to identify the amyloid type
  • Now often reserved for cases where non-invasive testing is inconclusive

Genetic Testing

Genetic testing is essential to differentiate between hereditary and wild-type ATTR-CM:

  • Sequencing of the TTR gene to identify mutations
  • Important for family screening in hereditary cases
  • Influences treatment decisions
